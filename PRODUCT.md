## Users

**Guests** — Indian travelers (weekend trippers, families, business travelers, workation remote workers, religious/heritage tourists) booking homestays, villas, farm stays, and heritage homes. Currently burned by OTAs: hidden fees inflating a ₹4,000 listing to ₹6,000–7,000, fake listings, inconsistent quality, last-minute cancellations. Job to be done: find a stay they can trust before they pay, at the price actually shown, and reach a real host directly.

**Hosts** — Property owners ranging from first-time homestay hosts to villa/farm-stay operators and property managers, concentrated initially in Gurgaon/Delhi NCR then tourist belt (Goa, Jaipur, Manali, Rishikesh, Udaipur, Coorg) and pan-India. Currently paying 10–20% commission to OTAs with opaque ranking/pricing algorithms and little local support. Job to be done: list a property, get found, keep most of what the guest pays, and get help with things like GST and onboarding without needing a tech team.

**Admin/Ops** — Internal team running the verification pipeline (identity, property, video, physical inspection) and city-launch operations across GTM phases.

## Product Purpose

Kiphaus is India's verified marketplace for homestays, villas, and unique stays. It replaces the commission-heavy, trust-poor OTA model with direct host-to-guest bookings, transparent all-in pricing, a zero-commission host economics model (hosts pay a flat annual subscription, not a per-booking cut), and a four-level verification framework that makes trust visible rather than assumed. Success = verified listings that convert guests, hosts renewing their subscription because direct bookings actually grow, and Kiphaus becoming the default answer to "is this stay actually legit."

## Brand Personality

Trustworthy, transparent, direct. Kiphaus should read like an institution you'd trust with a deposit, not a vacation brochure — confidence and rigor first, wanderlust second. The emotional target for guests is reassurance ("this is real, this is priced honestly"); for hosts it's respect ("this platform is on my side, not skimming me").

## Anti-references

- Airbnb / Booking.com / MakeMyTrip's fee-reveal-at-checkout pattern — total price must be transparent from the first screen.
- Generic warm-cream, illustration-heavy "vacation brochure" travel-brand visual clichés.
- Opaque host-facing ranking/pricing algorithms with no explanation.
- Platform-as-gatekeeper messaging systems that bury the host behind a "contact host" wall — the relationship should feel direct (WhatsApp-native), not platform-mediated.

## Design Principles

1. **Verification is the hero, not photography.** Trust badges and verification level are first-class UI, not fine print — they carry as much weight as the hero image.
2. **Direct-to-host, always.** Every guest-facing surface foregrounds the real host and a real channel (WhatsApp), never a faceless platform inbox.
3. **Transparent pricing everywhere.** The price shown at search is the price at booking. No fee reveal at the last step.
4. **Institutional trust register over travel-brochure register.** The existing navy/seafoam banking-grade visual language is a deliberate differentiator — it signals credibility the way a bank statement does, not the way a postcard does.
5. **Built for the full host spectrum.** From a first-time homestay owner to a multi-property villa manager — onboarding and dashboards must not assume tech fluency.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA baseline across all guest and host surfaces. Mobile-first (majority of both guest and host traffic is phone-based). Keyboard and screen-reader support required on every booking-critical flow: search, property page, booking/inquiry action, host listing wizard, verification status tracker.
